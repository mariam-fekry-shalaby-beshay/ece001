Programming languages history

In 1883, when a woman named Ada Lovelace worked with Charles Babbage on his very early mechanical computer, the Analytical Engine, he created his first computer programming language. Although Babbage was concerned with calculating numbers clearly, Lovelace saw that the numbers in which the machine operated could represent anything more than pure quantities of items. She wrote an Analytical Engine algorithm, which was the first of its kind. Lovelace gets credited with creating the first computer programming language because of her contribution. As different needs emerged and new devices were developed, a lot more languages followed.

Computer Programming Languages Today

Most computer programming languages were inspired by concepts from previous computer programming languages or built upon them. While older languages still serve as a strong foundation for new ones today, newer computer programming languages simplify the work of programmers. Businesses rely heavily on programs to satisfy all their data, transactions and customer service needs. For their study, science and medicine need specific and complex programmes. Mobile apps need to be updated to meet consumer demands. And all of these new and growing needs ensure that computer programming languages, both old and new, will remain an important part of modern life.
